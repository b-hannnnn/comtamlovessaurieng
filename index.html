The Crisis

Vietnam's durian
oversupply disaster

A boom in durian export prices triggered a mass planting frenzy. Farmers doubled their land, quadrupled production. Then China tightened import standards — and the entire industry collapsed.

Exports fell 80% in early 2025. Unsold fruit means mountains of husks. And nobody has a plan for them.

→
Export prices surged — farmers planted aggressively, total area doubled the government's own 2030 target
→
China tightened standards — only 25,000 of 151,000 ha have certified export codes
→
Oversupply & price crash — 30–40% of harvested fruit rejected, sold for almost nothing
→
Husk mountains — 60–70% of each fruit is husk, farmers pay 800,000đ/tonne to dispose of it

The fiber hiding in plain waste

Durian husk cellulose has mechanical properties comparable to cotton and hemp — tensile strength, elongation at break, moisture absorption. The raw material has been sitting in Vietnam's fields for decades. We're just the first to use it.

Lab residue (lignin, pectin) is processed into organic fertilizer and returned to the farms — closing the loop entirely.
